\noindent\textit{This paper supersedes the March 2026 draft on GraphRAG-to-Epistract, which described the v1 framework as a single-domain (drug-discovery) system. The current paper documents the v3.2 framework: a two-layer architecture with a categorical epistemic super-domain layer, four pre-built domains with domain-specific epistemic rules, and a single-source-of-truth analyst persona pattern that drives both reactive chat and proactive briefings. The earlier paper is preserved at \texttt{paper/main.tex} in the repository.}

\caption{Epistract on one page. \textbf{Layer 1, the brute facts}: a force-directed labeled property graph with entities (compounds, diseases, mechanisms, biomarkers, clinical trials) extracted from a corpus and color-coded by entity type, connected by typed directional relations. \textbf{Layer 2, the epistemic super-domain}: a categorical status (\texttt{asserted}, \texttt{prophetic}, \texttt{hypothesized}, \texttt{contested}, \texttt{contradiction}, \texttt{negative}) annotated on every relation by a rule engine that combines regex patterns over evidence text with document-type inference. \textbf{The analyst persona} (right): a single \texttt{persona.yaml} string per domain drives both the reactive workbench chat and a proactive auto-generated briefing (\texttt{epistemic\_narrative.md}) -- one source of truth, two surfaces. \textbf{The four pre-built domains} (bottom): drug-discovery, contracts, clinicaltrials, fda-product-labels. \textbf{Track record} (S1--S7): scenario corpora that have pressure-tested each domain.}
