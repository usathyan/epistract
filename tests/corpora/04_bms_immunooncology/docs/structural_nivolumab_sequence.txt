Title: Nivolumab (Opdivo) — Structural and Sequence Profile
Source: Compiled from DrugBank DB09035, IMGT, and published structure data

COMPOUND IDENTITY:
Nivolumab (BMS-936558, MDX-1106, ONO-4538, Opdivo) is a fully human IgG4
monoclonal antibody targeting PD-1 (programmed death-1, CD279).
Developed by the originator companies.

TARGET: PD-1 (PDCD1 gene, UniProt: Q15116)
PD-1 is a type I transmembrane receptor expressed on activated T cells, B cells,
and myeloid cells. Binding of PD-L1 (CD274) or PD-L2 (PDCD1LG2) to PD-1 delivers
inhibitory signals that suppress T cell activation.

Nivolumab blocks the PD-1/PD-L1 interaction with KD = 2.6 nM (BIAcore SPR assay).

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
By blocking PD-1, nivolumab prevents the inhibitory checkpoint signal, allowing
T cells to maintain anti-tumor activity. This represents immune checkpoint blockade —
a mechanism that does not directly target tumor cells but rather restores the
immune system's ability to recognize and destroy them.

KEY CLINICAL TRIALS:
- CheckMate 037 (NCT01721746): Phase III, melanoma post-CTLA-4
- CheckMate 067 (NCT01844505): Phase III, nivo+ipi vs nivo vs ipi, melanoma
  5-year OS: 52% (nivo+ipi), 44% (nivo), 26% (ipi)
- CheckMate 227 (NCT02477826): Phase III, nivo+ipi vs chemo, NSCLC
- RELATIVITY-047 (NCT03470922): Phase II/III, nivo+relatlimab vs nivo, melanoma
  PFS HR 0.75 (p=0.006)

BIOMARKERS:
- PD-L1 expression (TPS >= 1% or >= 50%): predictive of nivolumab response
- TMB-high (>= 10 mut/Mb): associated with improved response across tumor types
- MSI-H/dMMR: FDA-approved biomarker for nivolumab in certain settings

ADVERSE EVENTS:
Most common irAEs: fatigue (26%), rash (17%), pruritus (14%), diarrhea (13%)
Serious irAEs: immune-mediated pneumonitis (3.1%), colitis (1.7%), hepatitis (1.8%),
nephritis (1.2%), endocrinopathies - hypothyroidism (8.0%), hyperthyroidism (3.0%)
