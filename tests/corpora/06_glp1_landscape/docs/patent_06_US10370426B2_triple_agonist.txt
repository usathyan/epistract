Title: Triple glucagon/GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist
Source: Google Scholar → Google Patents
Patent ID: US10370426B2
Assignee: Hanmi Pharmaceutical Co Ltd
Filing Date: June 29, 2018

ABSTRACT/DESCRIPTION:
The invention concerns a triple agonist peptide possessing activities to all of glucagon, GLP-1, and GIP receptors, and its applications in treating metabolic disorders including obesity, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

The patent discloses 102 specific peptide variants (SEQ ID NOS: 1-102) for triple receptor agonism. The general peptide formula (SEQ ID NO: 103) is:
Xaa1-Xaa2-Xaa3-Gly-Thr-Phe-Xaa7-Ser-Asp-Xaa10-Ser-Xaa12-Xaa13-Xaa14-Xaa15-Xaa16-Xaa17-Xaa18-Xaa19-Xaa20-Xaa21-Phe-Xaa23-Xaa24-Trp-Leu-Xaa27-Xaa28-Xaa29-Xaa30-R1

Extended 40-amino-acid variants (SEQ ID NO: 104) and 39-amino-acid variants (SEQ ID NO: 105) with fixed positions are also described. Critical variable positions include residues 1, 2, 3, 7, 10, 12-24, 27-30, with variable side-chain identities enabling optimization of selectivity ratios across the three receptors (GLP-1R, GIPR, GCGR).

The triple agonist approach simultaneously activates glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor for glucose-dependent insulin secretion and appetite suppression, glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) receptor for enhanced insulin response and lipid metabolism, and glucagon receptor (GCGR) for increased energy expenditure and hepatic lipid oxidation. This multi-receptor mechanism produces synergistic weight loss and metabolic improvement beyond what single or dual agonists achieve.

The peptides may be modified with fatty acid conjugation or PEGylation at lysine residues to extend plasma half-life. Retatrutide (LY3437943), developed by Eli Lilly, is a related triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon receptor agonist currently in Phase 3 clinical trials that has demonstrated up to 24% body weight reduction in Phase 2 studies.
