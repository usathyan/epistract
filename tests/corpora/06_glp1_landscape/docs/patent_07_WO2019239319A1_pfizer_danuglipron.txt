Title: GLP-1 receptor agonists and uses thereof (benzimidazole series)
Source: Google Scholar → Google Patents
Patent ID: WO2019239319A1
Assignee: Pfizer Inc
Filing Date: June 11, 2019

ABSTRACT/DESCRIPTION:
The patent describes 6-carboxylic acids of benzimidazoles and 4-aza-, 5-aza-, and 7-aza-benzimidazoles as small-molecule GLP-1 receptor agonists, processes to make said compounds, and methods comprising administering said compounds to a mammal in need thereof. This patent is part of the chemical series from which danuglipron (PF-06882961) was developed by Pfizer.

Key compounds described include:

Compound 1: 2-({4-[2-(4-chloro-2-fluorophenyl)-1,3-benzodioxol-4-yl]piperidin-1-yl}methyl)-1-[(2S)-oxetan-2-ylmethyl]-1H-benzimidazole-6-carboxylic acid

Compound 2: 2-({4-[(2S)-2-(4-chloro-2-fluorophenyl)-2-methyl-1,3-benzodioxol-4-yl]piperidin-1-yl}methyl)-1-[(2S)-oxetan-2-ylmethyl]-1H-benzimidazole-6-carboxylic acid (including tris salt forms)

Compound 3: 2-({4-[2-(5-Chloropyridin-2-yl)-2-methyl-1,3-benzodioxol-4-yl]piperidin-1-yl}methyl)-1-[(2S)-oxetan-2-ylmethyl]-1H-benzimidazole-6-carboxylic acid (DIAST-X2)

These nonpeptide compounds activate the GLP-1 receptor through an allosteric mechanism, binding within a transmembrane pocket distinct from the orthosteric peptide-binding domain. They are orally bioavailable and resistant to proteolytic degradation by DPP-4 and neutral endopeptidases.

Therapeutic indications include type 2 diabetes mellitus, obesity, non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH), cardiovascular disease, and related metabolic disorders. Danuglipron, derived from this chemical series, advanced to Phase 2/3 clinical trials. Pfizer discontinued the twice-daily formulation but continues development of a once-daily extended-release formulation for obesity.
