Title: GIP/GLP1 agonist compositions
Source: Google Patents
Patent ID: US11357820B2
Assignee: Eli Lilly and Company
Filing Date: June 14, 2019
CAS Registry: 2023788-19-2

ABSTRACT/DESCRIPTION:
A pharmaceutical composition of tirzepatide, comprising an agent selected from NaCl and propylene glycol, and dibasic sodium phosphate is provided. Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist peptide with CAS Registry Number 2023788-19-2.

The tirzepatide peptide sequence (SEQ ID NO: 1) is: YX1EGTFTSDYSIX2LDKIAQKAFVQWLMGGPSSGAPPPS, where X1 = Aib (2-aminoisobutyric acid) and X2 = Aib. At position 20, the lysine (K) is chemically modified via conjugation to the epsilon-amino group with a C20 fatty diacid moiety: (2-[2-(2-Amino-ethoxy)-ethoxy]-acetyl)2-(gamma-Glu)1-CO-(CH2)18-CO2H. The C-terminal amino acid is amidated as a primary amide. This 39-amino-acid synthetic peptide is based on the native GIP sequence with modifications that confer dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist activity.

The fatty diacid moiety enables albumin binding, extending the plasma half-life to approximately 5 days and permitting once-weekly subcutaneous dosing. Tirzepatide has demonstrated superior efficacy in glycemic control (HbA1c reduction) and weight loss compared to selective GLP-1 receptor agonists in the SURPASS clinical trial program. The dual agonist mechanism activates both the GIP receptor (primary) and the GLP-1 receptor, with an imbalanced potency favoring GIP receptor activation.

Pharmaceutical compositions for subcutaneous injection comprise tirzepatide at concentrations suitable for therapeutic dosing, NaCl or propylene glycol as isotonic agent, and dibasic sodium phosphate as buffer, formulated for acceptable shelf-life stability. The tirzepatide trifluoroacetate salt form is also described.
