Title: Use of long-acting GLP-1 peptides
Source: Google Patents
Patent ID: US10335462B2
Assignee: Novo Nordisk AS
Filing Date: July 21, 2017
CAS Registry: N/A

ABSTRACT/DESCRIPTION:
The invention addresses therapeutic applications of extended-duration GLP-1 peptides through specific dosing protocols. A key embodiment involves administering a GLP-1 agonist with a minimum half-life of 72 hours in doses of at least 0.7 mg weekly (or semaglutide-equivalent) on a once-weekly or less frequent schedule for managing type 2 diabetes, hyperglycemia, glucose tolerance disorders, and obesity.

Semaglutide is described as a unique acylated GLP-1 peptide with a half-life of approximately 160 hours (approximately 7 days), prepared per Example 4 of WO2006/097537. The native GLP-1(7-37) peptide sequence is HAEGTFTSDVSSYLEGQAAKEFIAWLVKGRG (SEQ ID NO: 1). The key semaglutide analog is designated [Aib8, Arg34, Lys37]GLP-1(7-37), denoting substitutions where alpha-aminoisobutyric acid replaces the amino acid at position 8, arginine replaces the amino acid at position 34, and lysine replaces the amino acid at position 37. The peptide is further modified by acylation at Lys26 with a C18 fatty diacid through a gamma-glutamic acid and mini-PEG linker.

The long half-life of semaglutide compared to native GLP-1 (half-life approximately 2 minutes) and liraglutide (half-life approximately 13 hours) enables once-weekly dosing. Clinical studies demonstrated that weekly semaglutide at doses of 0.5 mg and 1.0 mg achieved significant reductions in HbA1c and body weight in patients with type 2 diabetes. The SUSTAIN clinical trial program showed cardiovascular safety with a trend toward reduced major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE).

The dosing regimen includes an escalation period starting at 0.25 mg weekly for 4 weeks, followed by 0.5 mg weekly, with optional further escalation to 1.0 mg weekly based on clinical response.
