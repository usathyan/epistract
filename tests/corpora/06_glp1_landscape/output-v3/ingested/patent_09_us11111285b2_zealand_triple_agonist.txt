Title: Glucagon-GLP-1-GIP triple agonist compounds
Source: Google Patents (via SerpAPI)
Patent ID: US11111285B2
Assignee: Zealand Pharma AS
Filing Date: October 4, 2018

ABSTRACT/DESCRIPTION:
The invention describes compounds possessing agonist activity at glucagon, GIP, and GLP-1 receptors for treating metabolic disorders including obesity, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. These triple agonists combine benefits across three receptor pathways in a single peptide molecule.

The core peptide backbone follows General Formula I (SEQ ID NO: 52):
R1-Tyr-X2-Gln-Gly-Thr-Phe-Thr-Ser-Asp-X10-Ser-X12-X13-Leu-X15-X16-X17-Ala-X19-X20-X21-Phe-X23-X24-Trp-Leu-X27-X28-X29-X30-Y1-R2

Key variable positions include:
- X2: Aib, Gly, Ala, D-Ala, Ser, N-Me-Ser, Ac3c, Ac4c, or Ac5c
- X10: Tyr or Leu (determines glucagon receptor potency)
- X12, X16, X17, X20, X24, X28: Can be Lys, Arg, Orn, or Cys with lipophilic conjugates for half-life extension

Representative sequences:
SEQ ID NO: 1 (wild-type reference): YSQGTFTSDYSKYLDSKAAHDFVEWLLRA
SEQ ID NO: 5: Y-Aib-QGTFTSDYSIYLDSKAAHDFVEWLLSA

Six C-terminal extension sequences (Y1, SEQ ID NOs 53-58) are derived from Exendin-4, including Gly-Pro-Ser-Ser-Gly-Ala-Pro-Pro-Pro-Ser (SEQ ID NO: 53). These extensions enhance in vivo stability without reducing glucagon receptor activity.

Lipophilic modification at designated residues uses fatty acid conjugates with terminal polar groups (carboxylic acid, phosphonate, or sulfonate) connected via PEG-containing spacers. This enables albumin binding and extends plasma half-life to support once-weekly or less frequent dosing.

The triple agonist mechanism simultaneously engages: GLP-1 receptor for glucose-dependent insulin secretion and appetite suppression; GIP receptor for enhanced insulin response and improved lipid metabolism; and glucagon receptor for increased energy expenditure and hepatic lipid oxidation. Zealand Pharma is developing these compounds as potential best-in-class treatments for obesity and metabolic syndrome.
